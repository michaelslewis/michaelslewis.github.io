JSON and the query response

On the previous page, we modified our search in an attempt to retrieve information on Tian Fu Noodle, located in Corvallis. Until now, the response to that request, and especially to the earlier 'food' query, have appeared as lengthy blobs of semi-formatted text that is relatively difficult to read. On this page, we will attempt to decipher those results.

I had initially noticed the Tian Fu Noodle restaurant as its name was the very first result out of 289 businesses that were returned in our original 'food' query. As an e-Campus student and a noodle-lover, I would like to know more about this restaurant for any visits I may make to Oregon State University in the future. The data that is returned to us from our query arrives in the JSON format, which, although not identical, is very close to the JavaScript Object Notation that we used to formulate the parameters of our queries.
